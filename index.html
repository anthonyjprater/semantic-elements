What's the Deal with Semantics?

You need them. You just don't know it yet.

So you're sitting in HTML 100 trying to enumerate on the differences between Git and Github... when suddenly a classmate ask the instructor about semantics in HTML5.
Now hang on and don't log out of the Zoom meeting just, yet. Semantics can give you a new outlook on development. Or lack thereof could land you into the world of divitis.

"Divitis is a common problem with webdesigners. It is the process of using too many nested/unnecessary divs to mark up a page."

Content Driven Highway

The beauty of semantic elements is the easy road map which they lay for your web design process to be content driven.
Let's be honest a beautifully styled web page can become pretty useless if the content isn't easily understood or accessible.

I Know You Know, But How Do I Know?

Accessibilty is arguably the second best thing about using semantic elements to markup your website.
You want visitors to be able to interact with your site in the most basic ways as possible and then work it out in your design process. Which would be easier to identify
and style because of the use of semantic elements and would give team members a story without the need for bread crumbs when collaborating.

The benefits extend beyond basic interaction to:

  1. Easier to develop
  2. Better on mobile devices
  3. Good for SEO
